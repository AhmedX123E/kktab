Our Story

From a Village Kitchen to the World

Hi, my name is Aicha. I'm a young Moroccan woman from the Souss-Massa region in southern Morocco, where argan trees grow on the hills and my mother has been cooking these exact recipes since before I was born.

I wrote these books because most books about Morocco are written by tourists who spend five days in Marrakech. I wanted to share the Morocco I actually grew up in — the one our grandmothers taught us, far from the airport postcards.

Every book is a piece of my heart. Every recipe is a memory. Every page is an invitation into a world that cannot be found in any guidebook.

Moroccan Cooking Made Simple

Learn how to cook authentic Moroccan food at home — from classic chicken tagine with preserved lemon and olives, to sweet-savory lamb tagine with prunes and almonds, to the famous Friday 7-vegetable couscous. Our cookbook includes harira soup for Ramadan, flaky msemen bread, crusty khobz, soft batbout, golden harcha, crispy maakouda, sweet chebakia cookies, and the legendary chicken pastilla. Every recipe includes both US cups and metric grams, and works in regular pots and pans — no clay tagine needed.

Discover Moroccan Culture & Heritage

Explore the rich cultural heritage of Morocco — from the ancient Amazigh (Berber) people and their traditions, to the four imperial cities of Fez, Marrakech, Meknes, and Rabat. Learn about zellige mosaic tilework, tadelakt lime plaster walls, Moroccan carpet weaving, handcrafted caftans, henna art, bakhoor incense, the khamsa hand, and the evil eye. Discover the unwritten rules of Moroccan mint tea ceremony, the hammam bathing tradition, Ramadan customs, Moroccan hospitality, and the full Moroccan national anthem in Arabic with English translation. This is Morocco as Moroccans actually live it — not the tourist version.
